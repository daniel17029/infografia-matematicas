Introduction
The National Open and Distance University (UNAD), together with nine prestigious universities from Colombia, Mexico, and Chile, organized the First International Congress of Interactive Mathematics Conferences. This virtual academic event united renowned researchers and professors to share innovative methodologies for teaching mathematics through digital tools, gamification, and STEM approaches — covering Logic, Algebra, Calculus, and expert panels on STEM applications.

⏰ 8:30 AM — Keynote conference
Active Methodologies and Universal Design for Learning as a Pedagogical Framework for Teaching Mathematics in STEM Environments
👤 Dr. Eilen Oviedo González — Mexico · Universidad Autónoma de Baja California (UABC)
STEM
The conference argues that the Universal Design for Learning (UDL) and active methodologies — project-based learning, intentional gamification, and flipped classroom — form the necessary pedagogical framework for teaching mathematics. The central thesis: technology alone does not guarantee learning; pedagogical design does. Dr. Oviedo González holds a PhD in Educational Management and Policy, with 40+ peer-reviewed articles and 6 published books.
⏰ 9:40 AM — Thematic conference · Logic
The DAE Method as an Interactive Strategy for the Development of Logical-Mathematical Thinking
👤 Dr. Leidy Hernández Mesa — Mexico · Universidad Autónoma de Baja California (UABC)
Logic
The DAE method (I Discover, I Learn, I Teach) is structured in three moments: self-discovery of prior knowledge, collective reflection and collaborative problem-solving, and argumentation of constructed knowledge. The approach strengthens analysis, argumentation, and critical reflection in mathematics students. Dr. Hernández Mesa is a full-time Professor-Researcher at UABC and creator of the DAE methodology for teacher training.

⏰ 2:00 PM — Thematic conference · Algebra
Papiroflexia and Algebraic Thinking: From Folding to Abstraction
👤 Dr. Estefanía Bravo Rubio — Chile · Universidad Técnica Federico Santa María
Algebra
This talk proposes origami (paper folding) as a didactic strategy that bridges the concrete and the abstract. Through folding, students analyze regularities, invariants, and relationships, enabling the transition toward formal algebraic representations such as functions and structural patterns.
⏰ 3:00 PM — Thematic conference · Calculus
Calculus from a Socioepistemological Modeling Category with Digital Technologies: GeoGebra, Sensors and Graphing Calculators in the Classroom
👤 Dr. María Esther Magali Méndez Guevara — Mexico · Universidad Autónoma de Guerrero
Calculus
The conference presents a Socioepistemological framework for teaching calculus through mathematical modeling. It demonstrates how GeoGebra, physical sensors, and graphing calculators help students resignify polynomial functions and understand the variation and behavior of variables in real-world contexts. Dr. Méndez Guevara is a Doctor in Mathematics Education (CINVESTAV-IPN) and member of SNIl Level I.

Conclusion
The I International Congress of Interactive Mathematics Conferences demonstrated that the future of mathematics education lies in the integration of active pedagogies, digital tools, and collaborative research. From the UDL framework to the DAE method, from origami-based algebra to GeoGebra-assisted calculus modeling, each conference reinforced a powerful idea: mathematics becomes meaningful when it is taught through experience, creativity, and technology. This event united universities from three countries under a shared commitment to transforming how mathematics is taught and learned at the higher education level.
